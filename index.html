Characteristic Load Estimator
for Aerial Acrobatics Rigging Equipment

Disclaimer:

This is just an estimate of possible dynamic loads in a rigging system that travels straight down from an anchor point. This does not take into account orbital motion which puts different strains on rigging equipment. For a more accurate estimate use a load cell sensor while running your act to determine your actual dynamic loads. Take the peak load from running your act using the load cell sensor and multiply by 6 to get your characteristic load.

Methodology:

(Weight of performers + weight of apparatus & rigging equipment) × dynamic adjustment factor × safety factor of 6 (from ANSI 1.43 2016 Entertainment Technology — Performer Flying Systems Subsection 4.6.5).

Example:

(440lbs performers + 10lbs trapeze) × 3 (dynamic adjustment factor) × 6 (ANSI safety factor) = 8100lbs. max safety trapeze performers

Safety Notes:

Many times the weakest point in an aerial rigging system is the anchor point. Carefully examine your anchor point and trace the forces from the point of attachment to the main support structure down to the ground. Get assistance from a professional rigger or structural engineer if necessary. When in doubt of the structural integrity of the anchor point, Do Not Perform.
